Final Project Report: Navier-Stokes GLSL Fluid Simulation and Deep Learning Application

Praveen Batra Philippe Hansen-Estruch, Dean Zhang, Anthony Ding

Abstract

A 1-2 page summary of the entire project.

In this project, we implemented a GLSL shader pipeline on the staff Vulkan shader graph for rendering fluid dynamics simulations with mouse or programmatic input. Our pipeline used the grid-based approximation to the Navier-Stokes equations outlined in Jos Stam's landmark paper "Real-Time Fluid Dynamics for Games."

We also implemented a variational autoencoder (VAE) and MLP dyanmics model for next-frame prediction using one of our fluid simulations as training data to see if a deep learning-based approach could learn fluid dynamics. However, we found that the VAE and dynamics model together learned a degenerate latent space, so we had to use an end-to-end technique where the VAE also predicted the next frame. Ultimately, the machine learning technique was limited by a lack of training data and compute time, but we believe it has some potential.
